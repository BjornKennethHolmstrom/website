_A Brutally Honest Autopsy of the Monopoly on Legitimate Violence_

## Overview

This capstone paper examines the modern nation-state as the final architecture binding together the illusions we've explored. It dissects the state's claim to ultimate authority through the central fiction of unified popular sovereignty - the myth that the state embodies the "will of the people."

The analysis shows how the state's illusion of power as legitimacy enables market externalities and religious nationalism to persist. It explores post-sovereign alternatives and fosters conscious citizenship that navigates the tension between collective action and concentrated power.
